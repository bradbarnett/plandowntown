The past decade has brought new park spaces, transit, hotels, office buildings, restaurants and residential developments that have transformed the character of Downtown, but what's next? Plan Downtown is a new 20-year vision that will position Downtown at the forefront in leading Houston's advancement as a great global city.

Project Background

In the summer of 2016, the Houston Downtown Management District, along with the following organizations, identified the need to prepare and issue a new Development Framework or plan for Downtown Houston:

  • Houston First Corporation
  • Central Houston, Inc.
  • Downtown Redevelopment Authority / TIRZ No.3
  • Buffalo Bayou Partnership
  • City of Houston
  • Harris County
  • Theater District Houston
Board Members from these organizations plus Board Members from neighboring Management Districts — East Downtown, Greater East End, Greater Northside and Midtown — comprise a Leadership Group who will guide the Framework process from inception to final delivery. The Framework will serve these sponsoring and neighboring organizations and a broad range of stakeholders as a 20-year “vision plan” leading towards development recommendations and implementation of public and private improvements and initiatives within and connected to Downtown. The geographic area for planning consideration is generally bounded by Interstate 10, 45 and 69 and with adjacent stakeholder collaboration for connections to adjacent districts including East Downtown, Greater East End, Greater Northside and Midtown neighborhoods as well as portions of Houston’s Historic First and Sixth Wards.

With the advancements made since the 2004 Houston Downtown Development Framework (available for download: http://www.downtownhouston.org/development/planning/) the goal of the Leadership Group is to develop a plan that positions Downtown at the forefront in leading Houston’s advancement as a great global city.

About the Project

The Plan Downtown process will develop broad and far-reaching strategies with a visionary mindset to transform Downtown Houston and the surrounding neighborhoods for a competitive and enriched future and will build up the goals and core strategies in Plan Houston, the new General Plan adopted by City Council in fall 2015 by engaging a broad public in the development of the plan.

Plan Downtown will focus on answering “the big questions” within nine interrelated topics:

  • Build Downtown: What are the competitive advantages of Downtown and how do we build on those in the future?
  • Green Downtown: How do we take advantage of and extend networks of green space Downtown?
  • Connect Downtown: How do we overcome barriers that divide Downtown from its adjacent neighborhoods?
  • Move Downtown: How do we balance the needs of commuters with the desire for more livable streets?
  • Work Downtown: How do we attract the users of tomorrow to Downtown?
  • Visit Downtown: What makes Downtown a competitive destination for regional, national, and international visitors?
  • Live Downtown: Who are the residents of Downtown, and who are we trying to attract in the future?
  • Play Downtown: How can we replicate and complement existing successful public spaces?
  • Learn Downtown: How do we leverage schools and universities to drive Downtown innovation and attract new office and residential populations?

The Plan Downtown process will include numerous methods of getting involved, including text and online surveys, public events, tours, topical focus groups and charrettes with the adjoining neighborhoods.
